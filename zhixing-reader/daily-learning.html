Understanding CBT

认知行为疗法入门 · 约 8 分钟

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, goal-oriented form of psychotherapy that helps individuals identify and change distorted thought patterns. Developed by Aaron Beck in the 1960s, it has become one of the most widely practiced and empirically supported therapeutic approaches.

The core premise of CBT is that our psychology is shaped by the interplay between thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. When someone experiences rumination or cognitive distortion, these maladaptive patterns can lead to anxiety and depression.

Through consistent practice, patients develop resilience and learn to reframe negative automatic thoughts. The brain's neuroplasticity allows these new patterns to become habitual over time.

认知行为疗法（CBT）是一种结构化、目标导向的心理治疗形式，帮助个体识别和改变扭曲的思维模式。由亚伦·贝克于20世纪60年代提出，现已成为最广泛实践且最有实证支持的治疗方法之一。

CBT的核心前提是，我们的心理由思维、情绪和行为之间的相互作用塑造。当一个人经历反刍思维或认知扭曲时，这些适应不良的模式可能导致焦虑和抑郁。

通过持续练习，患者发展出心理韧性，并学会重构消极的自动思维。大脑的神经可塑性使这些新模式随时间成为习惯。

The Science of Habits

习惯的科学 · 约 6 分钟

Habits are the brain's way of saving energy. When a behavior is repeated, the brain releases dopamine, reinforcing the action through a cue-routine-reward loop.

Understanding this loop is the first step to changing any habit. By keeping the cue and reward but swapping the routine, we can reshape behavior over time.

Mindfulness in Daily Life

日常生活中的正念 · 约 7 分钟

Mindfulness is the practice of paying attention to the present moment without judgment. Research shows it can reduce stress and improve focus.
